Chapter: Company Formation

1. Define Company.

Ans:- Company is a voluntary association of many persons for profit with capital divisible into transferable shares with limited liabilities, having corporate body, separate legal entity and a common seal for perpetual succession.

2. Explain any two features of a company

Ans:-
  • 1)Legal Entity: A company has a separate legal entity from its owners, allowing it to hold and deal with any property it owns, enter into contracts, open bank accounts, and take legal actions in its own name and capacity.
  • 2)Limited Liability: Shareholders in a company have limited liability up to the face value of the shares they hold, meaning they are not responsible for paying the excess claims of creditors if the company's capital is insufficient.

3. What is a public limited company?

Ans:- A public limited company is a type of company that raises capital by issuing shares to the public. According to the Nepal Company Act 2074 (Amended), a public company is one that is not a private company and must have at least seven members with no limitation on the maximum number. The shares of a public limited company are freely transferable.

4. Write any two privileges of a public company

Ans:-
  • 1)Ability to Raise Capital: A public limited company can raise a large amount of capital by issuing shares to the public.
  • 2)Transferable Shares: The shares of a public limited company are easily transferable from one person or party to another.

5. Define private company

Ans:- A private company is a type of company that does not issue shares to the public for raising capital. It can be formed with one member, but the maximum number of shareholders cannot exceed 100 and restrictions are placed on the transfer of shares from one shareholder to another.

7.Define chartered and statutory company.

Ans:- A company, which is incorporated or established under special charter or proclamation issued by the head of the state, is known as chartered company and a company which is formed or incorporated by a special act of parliament is known as statutory companies. Such company is governed by the respective acts and do not have any memorandum or articles of association.

8.Define registered company.

Ans:- A company which is formed according to company acts is called registered company. The provisions of company act, memorandum of association and articles of association govern such company.

9.Who are company promoters? Explain their functions.

Ans:- The person or a group of person who take necessary steps to incorporate a company are known as company promoters. They conceive the idea of starting the company and work up on it to develop the idea and finally form it.
  • Some of the functions are as follows:-
  • A. Conceive the idea of forming a company.
  • B. Analyze profitability and feasibility of the idea.
  • C.Investigate the workability of the idea by consulting experts.
  • D.Decide the name and location of the company and also the amount and form of its share capital.

10.What is MOA?

Ans:- Memorandum of association is the main document of the company for its incorporation which contents the fundamental rules regarding the activities of a company. Hence, A company cannot be registered without it. It sets out constitution of a company and the foundation on which the structure of the company is based.

11.Write any four contents of MOA.

Ans:-
  • The four contents of MOA are:-
  • A.The name of the company.
  • B.The address of the register office of the company.
  • C.The objectives of the company.
  • D.The acts to be carried out to accomplish the objectives of the company.
